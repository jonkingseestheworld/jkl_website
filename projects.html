This is a selected list of research and side projects I have recently worked on ...

POST-DECISION INFORMATION SEEKING

Sometimes we want to know 'what if' after making a decision, even though knowing the alternative outcomes may make us feel bad. In this line of work, Lily FitzGibbon, Kou Murayama and I have examined the neural mechanisms underlying information seeking behaviours of this sort. The related publication is in progress.

OCEAN PLASTIC BAG IMAGE CLASSIFIER

I trained my first proper deep learning model and built a simple image classifier web app that identifies plastic bags in the sea with the help of FastAI.
[LINK to the web app]

MUSIC MATCH MAKER

I built a song analyzer and recommendation tool using Spotify listening histories and song feature data extracted from Kaggle. I based it on Random Forest, and tested other ML models too.
[LINK to the scripts]

MOTIVATED RISKY DECISION-MAKING

In this project - a collaboration with Kou Murayama and other researchers in Japan - I demonstrated that decisions driven by curiosity share similar neural mechanisms as those motivated by instrumental rewards in a group of brain areas called the brain's reward network. This work has now been published in the journal Nature Human Behaviour.

CORTICAL LESION FINDER

In this ongoing project which started as a hackathon, we aimed to develop a free and open source voxel-based morphemetry solution to automate identification of epiletogenic lesions (using Python-based pipelines).
[LINK here]

NEUROHACKMAP

In the summer of 2019 I participated in a two-week hackathon at the University of Washington, Seattle, and contributed to a code project which generates interactve visualizations of geospatial data related to the participants' career movements.
[LINK to the project]

BRAIN ACTIVITY PATTERNS PREDICTED GAMBLE DECISIONS

This side project used multivariate pattern analysis (incorporating Support Vector Classification/ Multiple Kernel Learning models) of brain activity patterns to predict people's gambling decisions. This work was presented at the Organization for Human Brain Mapping conference.
